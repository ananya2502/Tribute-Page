LATA MANGESHKAR ACHIEVEMENTS

By the Government of India

Padma Bhushan,1969

The Madhya Pradesh government started a national level award to honour musicians for their contribution to Indian music. The award was named after Lata Mangeshkar, in an attempt to recognise her efforts to uplift Indian music.

Dadasaheb Phalke Award,1989

Widely credited as the "nightingale of India", playback singer Mangeshkar started her career in the 1942 and has sung songs in over 36 languages.

Bharat Ratna,2001

In 2001, in recognition of her contributions to the nation, she was awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honour; she is only the second female singer, after M. S.



National film awards

1972 - Best Female Playback Singer for songs of the film (parichay).

1972, Lata Mangeshkar won the first National Award for Best Female Playback Singer for the film Parichay.Over the years, the veteran singer won numerous national and international awards including the prestigious Bharat Ratna,

1974 - Best Female Playback Singer for songs of the film (Kora kagaz)

Kora Kagaz is a 1974 Indian Hindi-language drama film produced by Sanath Kothari.The famous title song "Mera Jeevan Kora Kagaz was rendered by Kishore Kumar.At the 22nd National Film Awards, it won the award for Best Popular Film, while Lata Mangeshkar won the award for Best Female Playback Singer.

1990 - Best Female Playback Singer for songs of the film Lekin.

In 1990 she launched her own production house for Hindi movies which produced the Gulzar-directed movie Lekin.... She won her third National Film Award for Best Female Playback Singer for her rendition of the song "Yaara Sili Sili" from the film, which was composed by her brother Hridaynath



Filmfare awards

1959 - “Aaja Re Pardesi” from Madhumati

Madhumati is a 1959 Indian Hindi-language paranormal romance film directed and produced by Bimal Roy. The film stars Vyjayanthimala and Dilip Kumar in lead roles, with Pran and Johnny Walker in supporting roles. The plot focuses on Anand, a modern man who falls in love with a tribal woman named Madhumati.

1963 – “Kahi Deep Jale Kahi Dil”

Kahin Deep Jale Kahin Dil is an Indian song from the Bollywood film Bees Saal Baad (1962). The lyrics of the song were written by Shrikhan. In 1963, Badayuni received the Filmfare Award for Best Lyricist and Lata Mangeshkar received the Filmfare Award for Best Female Playback Singer for this song.This was Mangeshkar's second Filmfare award

1994 –“Didi Tera Devar Deewana”

Didi Tera Devar Deewana" is a 1994 Hindi-language for the soundtrack of the 1994 Indian musical romantic drama film Hum Aapke Hain Koun..!. The music video of "Didi Tera Devar Deewana" shows the film's ensemble cast and leads Madhuri Dixit and Salman Khan dancing at a baby shower ceremony.
